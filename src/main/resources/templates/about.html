Welcome to FlatRateSucks

Keeping track of your flat rate hours is hard. We're here to make it easy.




This is the about page, as you may have noticed. Hopefully all of your questions will be answered in here. If not, let us know and we can fix that.


How does this thing work?

I'm sure you're aware, but keeping track of your hours at work isn't the easiest. We're here to help. Once you create an account and log in the rest should be pretty easy. There's a "dashboard" page that shows you your jobs listed by repair order. If you want to sort or search by other means, you can do that, too. There are some little buttons at the top of the table to help you out. Poke around and see what you think. When you need to add a job, click the button at the top of the page. Once you're there, fill out the spaces and save your job with out of the buttons on the top or bottom. Easy as that. Need to go back and look or edit a job? Click on the repair order number and you're good to go.



There are some important things to know.

We can't all be perfect. There are bound to be some times you run into an error page. Upon seeing that confusing screen, take some time for self-reflection and think about how your life has come to this point. Or, just hit the back button and see what the heck is breaking the website.


When it comes to tracking the facets of your jobs, there are some good things to know. The "Repair Order" field is for numbers only. Whole numbers to be specific. Don't try to get fancy and add letters or any punctuation, that's not how the database works. The "Hours" section is similar, but you can have decimals in that one. The date field should be pretty self-explanatory. The other fields can handle whatever you want to put in there...within reason. Keep it short and simple and everything should work just fine.



Ten lines? What gives?

Do you really need more? Obviously, in some cases, you do. We figured ten is a pretty safe bet for most of the repair orders you will want to track. When you run into the car with tons of lines, simply create another job with the same repair order number and add the rest the same way you added the first ten lines.
